Human Anatomy

Human anatomy is the scientific study of the body's structures. It includes the study of the body's organs, tissues, and cells.

Major Systems

  • Skeletal System: Provides structure and support to the body.
  • Muscular System: Allows movement through muscle contraction.
  • Circulatory System: Transports blood, nutrients, and oxygen throughout the body.
  • Respiratory System: Facilitates breathing and gas exchange.
  • Digestive System: Breaks down food and absorbs nutrients.
  • Nervous System: Controls and coordinates body activities through electrical signals.

Organs

Key organs include the heart, lungs, brain, liver, and kidneys, each performing vital functions necessary for survival.
