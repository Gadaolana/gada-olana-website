Human Anatomy

Explore the intricate structures that make up the human body and learn about the complex relationships between different anatomical systems.

Major Body Systems

  • Skeletal System: Provides structure, support, and protection for the body
  • Muscular System: Enables movement and maintains posture
  • Nervous System: Controls and coordinates body functions
  • Cardiovascular System: Transports blood, oxygen, and nutrients throughout the body
  • Respiratory System: Facilitates gas exchange between the body and the environment
  • Digestive System: Breaks down food and absorbs nutrients
  • Endocrine System: Produces hormones that regulate various bodily functions
  • Immune System: Defends the body against pathogens and foreign substances

Levels of Organization

Human anatomy can be studied at various levels of organization, from the smallest to the largest:

  1. Cellular level: Individual cells and their components
  2. Tissue level: Groups of similar cells working together
  3. Organ level: Structures composed of different tissues with specific functions
  4. Organ system level: Groups of organs working together to perform complex functions
  5. Organism level: The entire human body as a functioning unit

Anatomical Terminology

Understanding anatomical terminology is crucial for describing the human body accurately:

  • Directional terms: Superior, inferior, anterior, posterior, medial, lateral
  • Body planes: Sagittal, coronal, transverse
  • Body cavities: Cranial, thoracic, abdominal, pelvic
  • Anatomical positions: Supine, prone, anatomical position
