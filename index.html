Human Anatomy

Explore the intricate structures of the human body and discover how they work together to maintain life and health.

Skeletal System

The skeletal system provides structure, support, and protection for the body. It consists of bones, cartilage, and ligaments.

  • 206 bones in an adult human body
  • Produces red and white blood cells
  • Stores minerals like calcium and phosphorus

Muscular System

The muscular system is responsible for movement, posture, and heat production in the body.

  • Over 600 muscles in the human body
  • Three types: skeletal, smooth, and cardiac
  • Consumes about 20% of the body's energy at rest

Nervous System

The nervous system controls and coordinates all bodily functions and activities.

  • Divided into central and peripheral nervous systems
  • Contains approximately 86 billion neurons
  • Processes information at speeds up to 268 mph

Circulatory System

The circulatory system transports blood, oxygen, and nutrients throughout the body.

  • Heart pumps about 2,000 gallons of blood daily
  • Blood vessels could circle the Earth 2.5 times if laid out
  • Red blood cells live for about 120 days
